Hi, I'm Alyssa. Nice to meet you! I am a recent graduate of the University of California, Davis with a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science. The work put into obtaining these degrees have prepared me well by developing my working knowledge of a variety of programming languages and coding methods as well as honing my critical thinking and problem-solving skills.

In addition to my education, I have experience in the field. As a Web Developer for Game Observational Video Analysis (OVA), I developed and deployed their official website. This experience equipped me with a solid understanding of responsive design, web standards, and UI/UX best practices. At the Informational & Educational Technology Data Center, I honed my communication and organizational skills through weekly meetings about monitoring expectations as well as handling and resolving tickets to improve monitoring efficiency with programming. As a React Web Development Intern with CliQ, I learned the importance of staying up to date on the latest technologies in web development. Converting CliQ’s existing pages and features as well as bringing the designs and wireframes to life in React, made it easier to maintain and grow their codebase and improved the quality of their overall application. In all, my education and past experiences have sparked my interest in the following, but not limited to front end web development, mobile (iOS) development, programming languages, and artificial intelligence.

Some fun facts about me are that I proudly identify as part of the LGBTQIA+ community, I like to collect mechanical keyboards and vintage cameras, and I love coffee and tea. I am based in San Francisco and I am currently seeking entry-level positions as a software engineer in the Bay Area. While I don’t have years of experience under my belt, I believe my focus, hard-working personality, and eagerness to learn and grow in the industry would be an asset on any team.

Card Bank

Built for my ECS 189E course in Winter 2021. The app is a wallet, which can be broken down into two parts: login and wallet. For the login part, Card Bank uses phone number authentication. It takes in a phone number from the user and sends a verification code to the phone number by text message. Card Bank then prompts the user to enter the code and verify user's identity. For the wallet part, Card Bank displays the user's wallet, which has a table of accounts and the information of each account.
