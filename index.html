About

Alyssa Rodriguez

Hi, I'm Alyssa. Nice to meet you! I am a recent graduate of the University of California, Davis with a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science. Before transferring to UC Davis, I went to Diablo Valley College for three years to earn my Associate of Science degree. The work put into obtaining these degrees have prepared me well by developing my working knowledge of a variety of programming languages and coding methods as well as honing my critical thinking and problem-solving skills.

In addition to my education, I have experience in the field. As a Web Developer for Game Observational Video Analysis (OVA), I developed and deployed their official website. This experience equipped me with a solid understanding of responsive design, web standards, and UI/UX best practices. At the Information & Educational Technology Data Center, I honed my communication and organizational skills through weekly meetings about monitoring expectations as well as handling tickets to improve monitoring efficiency with programming. As a React Web Development Intern with CliQ, I learned the importance of staying up to date on the latest technologies in web development. Converting CliQ’s existing pages and features as well as bringing the designs and wireframes to life in React, made it easier to maintain and grow their codebase and improved the quality of their app. While I don’t have years of experience under my belt, I believe my focus, hard-working personality, and eagerness to learn and grow in the industry would be an asset on any team.

In all, my education and past experiences have sparked my interest in front end development for web applications. I grew a passion for building aesthetically pleasing user interfaces and unique user experiences with code. I have a strong proficiency in HTML5, CSS3, and JavaScript. I have a solid understanding in React and currently honing my skills in building reusable components.

Some fun facts about me are that I proudly identify as part of the LGBTQIA+ community, I like to collect keyboards and vintage cameras, and I love coffee and tea. I am based in San Francisco and I am currently seeking entry-level positions as a front end software engineer in the Bay Area.
